# No AI-generated imagery — the permanent rule

This rule is **non-negotiable**. It is the most important invariant for the product's trust wedge.

## Why

This product wins by being demonstrably more honest than generic AI about Vietnam. The moment a user finds an AI-generated photo of a "Vietnamese street" or "Huế royal courtyard" on this site, the brand collapses. They will (correctly) assume the facts are AI-generated too.

We compete on **"we were actually there"**. Photos are part of that.

## What counts as AI imagery

- Stable Diffusion / Midjourney / DALL-E / Imagen / Firefly outputs — banned
- "AI-enhanced" photos where AI added or removed objects — banned
- AI-generated illustrations of personas, places, or food — banned
- AI-upscaled photos where the upscaler hallucinated detail — banned
- GAN-generated faces of fictional people — banned

## What's allowed

- Photos taken by fixers during verification trips
- Photos licensed from named Vietnamese photographers (with credit shown on image hover/tap)
- User-submitted photos with verified provenance (EXIF + user attestation)
- Diagrams, icons, glyphs, charts, maps — generated by humans or non-image-AI tools
- Photo light-touch editing (crop, color, exposure) — yes, that's normal photography

## Where this is enforced

**4-checkpoint pipeline** (spec'd in `02-SPECS/content-corpora-and-fixer.md`):

1. **Upload-time EXIF allowlist** — uploads without a real camera EXIF signature OR uploads with known AI-generation markers (e.g. "Midjourney" / "Stable Diffusion" / "DALL-E" in metadata, specific neural-rendering signatures) are rejected at the API.
2. **pHash similarity check** — uploaded images compared against a corpus of known AI-generated images; high similarity → rejected.
3. **Manual fixer review** — every image visible to users passes through a fixer's queue. Fixers are trained to spot AI artifacts (waxy skin, melted hands, repeating textures, off-aspect-ratio diacritics on signs).
4. **CI guard** — `pnpm build` fails if any committed asset is flagged. Periodic re-scan of `public/` and R2 bucket.

## DB enforcement

The `asset` table (Stage 4 todo) will have:

```sql
provenance_kind text not null check (provenance_kind in (
  'fixer_camera',
  'licensed_photographer',
  'user_attestation'
));
ai_check_passed boolean not null default false;
fixer_review_signature_id uuid references fixer_signature(id);

-- assets only visible to users when both checks pass
create view public_asset as
  select * from asset
   where ai_check_passed = true
     and fixer_review_signature_id is not null;
```

## The egress allowlist

The TikTok share-card route (`/api/og`) only renders images from URLs matching the asset CMS allowlist. Even if a future bug let an AI image into R2, the share card render path would reject it. See [`02-SPECS/tiktok-share-cards.md`](https://github.com/DungMai/vietnam/blob/main/02-SPECS/tiktok-share-cards.md).

## What about persona character art?

In Direction A (the locked art direction for Phase 1 — see Studio `04-DESIGN/art-direction.md`), personas have NO illustrated mascots. Each province is differentiated by accent color + photographic mood, not by an AI-generated character.

In Direction B (Phase 2 upgrade — folk-modern aesthetic), each province MAY get a commissioned folk-art-influenced motif. These are **human-illustrated** by named Vietnamese illustrators with named credit. **Never AI-generated**.

## "But AI image quality is improving..."

No. This rule is **permanent**, not provisional. Even if AI images become indistinguishable, the trust commitment is: "every image you see is from a real place a real person photographed or drew." That's the deal. The day we break it is the day a competitor with that exact commitment eats our lunch.

## How to flag a violation

If you find an image on this product that looks AI-generated:

1. Open a security report per [`SECURITY.md`](../SECURITY.md) — this is a P0.
2. We will pull the image within 24h.
3. We will publish a postmortem (transparency is part of the trust).

## How to verify your own contribution

Before submitting an image via fact-correction or scam-report:

- Take the photo yourself, OR
- Get explicit permission from the photographer with attribution
- Include EXIF or a clear statement of provenance ("I took this on [date] with [device]")
- If you use any AI tool in your workflow (upscaling, denoising), state it; we'll evaluate case-by-case
